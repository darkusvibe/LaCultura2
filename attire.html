Kikuyu Garments

Made from animal skins, decorated with cowrie shells.

Kikuyu traditional attire features animal hides softened and decorated with shells and beads. Worn during rituals, weddings, and dances, it symbolizes beauty, fertility, and respect for nature.

Swahili Kanzu

A long white robe worn by Swahili men, often during ceremonies.

The Swahili Kanzu is a graceful ankle-length robe often paired with a kofia (cap). It represents modesty and elegance, especially during weddings and religious celebrations.

Luo Traditional Attire

Outfits adorned with beaded accessories.

Luo attire showcases bold fabrics and detailed beadwork symbolizing status and celebration. It reflects a proud heritage, often worn during cultural festivals and weddings.

Kalenjin Traditional Wear

Traditionally made from hides and adorned with beadwork.

The Kalenjin attire represents strength and maturity, especially during initiation rites. Beads, feathers, and cowhide signify bravery and connection to ancestral traditions.

Luhya Traditional Attire

Colorful outfits with cowrie shells and bead necklaces.

Luhya attire emphasizes community and unity. The bright fabrics and ornaments are worn in weddings and dances, expressing joy and cultural pride.

Kisii Traditional Attire

Attire made from hides or kitenge fabric, often with beadwork.

Kisii traditional wear combines earth tones and patterns symbolizing resilience and beauty. It includes beadwork, headdresses, and wraps worn during cultural festivals and ceremonies.
