Koitalel Arap Samoei

The Seer of Nandi A symbol of prophecy, leadership, and resistance.

Story Overview

Koitalel Arap Samoei was a revered leader and spiritual seer of the Nandi people. In the early 1900s, he foresaw the coming of the British and warned his people of their intentions to seize land and livestock.

Guided by his prophetic visions, Koitalel organized a powerful resistance movement against colonial intrusion, uniting the Nandi under a vision of sovereignty and dignity. His leadership was deeply spiritual he was believed to communicate with ancestral spirits, guiding his people through both war and faith.

“The land is our soul to lose it is to lose ourselves.”

In 1905, he was betrayed and killed during what was supposed to be a peace negotiation with the British. Today, Koitalel Arap Samoei remains a national hero and a symbol of Kenya’s resilience and freedom struggle.
